# ULTIMATE TEXAS HOLD'EM — PLAY BET BALANCE VALIDATION BUG

There is a critical betting bug in Ultimate Texas Hold'em.

The game is allowing the player to place a Play bet multiplier even when they do not have enough chips remaining to cover that wager.

This must be fixed immediately.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

# EXAMPLE BUG

Player bankroll:

3M chips

Player places:

Ante: 500K  
Blind: 500K  
Trips Bonus: 500K  

Total already committed:

1.5M

Remaining chips:

1.5M

Now the player reaches the pre-flop Play decision.

Valid options:

3x Play Bet = 1.5M

4x Play Bet = 2M

The player only has 1.5M remaining.

Therefore:

3x should be allowed.

4x should be disabled.

Current bug:

The game still allows 4x.

This is wrong.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

# REQUIRED RULE

Before showing any Play multiplier button, validate the player's remaining available chip balance.

Formula:

Available Chips = Current Balance - Already Committed Bets

Play Bet Cost = Ante × Multiplier

Only enable a Play multiplier if:

Available Chips >= Play Bet Cost

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

# BUTTON BEHAVIOR

If the player cannot afford a multiplier:

Disable that button.

Show it dimmed.

Do not allow tap.

Optional small label:

INSUFFICIENT CHIPS

Do not allow the player to go negative.

Do not allow hidden credit.

Do not allow the bet to be placed and corrected later.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

# APPLY TO ALL PLAY BET STAGES

This validation must apply to every Ultimate Texas Hold'em Play decision:

Pre-Flop:

3x  
4x  

After Flop:

2x  

River:

1x  

Every stage must check available chips before enabling the action.
