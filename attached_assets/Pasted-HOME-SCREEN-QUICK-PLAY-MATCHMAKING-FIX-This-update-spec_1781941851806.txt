HOME SCREEN QUICK PLAY MATCHMAKING FIX

This update specifically applies to the QUICK PLAY button located on the HOME SCREEN.

It does NOT apply to:

• Private Tables
• Tournaments
• Custom Games
• Future Event Modes

Only the HOME SCREEN QUICK PLAY feature.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

CURRENT ISSUE

When a player presses QUICK PLAY from the Home Screen, the system creates matches against opponents with dramatically smaller bankrolls.

Example:

Player:
13.1M Chips

Opponents:

Ace:
6.5K

Blaze:
8.4K

Shadow:
13.4K

Vegas:
18.4K

This should never happen.

A player with millions of chips should not be seated against opponents with only a few thousand chips.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

REQUIRED FIX

When a player presses QUICK PLAY from the Home Screen:

Step 1

Read the player's current bankroll.

Example:

13.1M

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 2

Determine the player's stake bracket.

Example:

13.1M

=
VIP Bracket

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 3

Generate opponents within the same bankroll range.

Example:

Player:
13.1M

AI Opponents:

8.7M

11.2M

15.6M

19.4M

Acceptable.

NOT ACCEPTABLE

Player:

13.1M

6.5K

8.4K

Generate AI bankrolls approximately between:

TABLE BUY-IN RULE

All players seated through HOME SCREEN QUICK PLAY must qualify for the same table.

Example:

100K / 200K Table

Minimum Buy-In:
2M

Maximum Buy-In:
20M

Every player at the table must fit within those limits.

LIVE PLAYER MATCHMAKING

If the player chooses:

🌎 LIVE PLAYERS

from the Home Screen Quick Play flow:

Match players using:

1. Selected blind level
2. Stake bracket
3. Buy-in range

Do not seat:

100K bankroll players

with

50M bankroll players

at the same table.

DESIGN GOAL

The Home Screen Quick Play button should always create realistic chip-matched games.

A player with millions of chips should play against opponents with millions of chips.

A player with thousands of chips should play against opponents with thousands of chips.

Every Quick Play game should feel like a legitimate casino table rather than a table filled with random opponents that could never afford the same stakes.
