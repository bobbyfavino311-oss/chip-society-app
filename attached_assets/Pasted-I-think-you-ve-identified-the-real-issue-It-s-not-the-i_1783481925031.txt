I think you’ve identified the real issue.

It’s not the icons anymore—they’re much better.

It’s the fact that they’re still inside cards. The cards are acting like giant buttons, but visually they don’t need to.

What I’d do instead (this is how Apple and high-end mobile games often handle it):

Turn the entire reward into a floating touch target, not a boxed card.

Imagine this:

        ◜──────────◝
          🎡
      (progress arc)
      DAILY SPIN
    ╭──────────────╮
      18h 05m
    ╰──────────────╯

No visible card.

No border.

No rectangle.

The player still taps anywhere in that area, but the touch target is invisible.

⸻

Invisible Touch Area

Instead of showing a box:

* Keep a large hit area (about 180×200 px).
* The entire region is tappable.
* The UI doesn’t reveal that hit area until interaction.

This is a common pattern in premium apps: large invisible touch targets with minimal visual chrome.

⸻

Press Animation

When the user taps anywhere in the reward area:

* The icon lifts 4 px.
* The progress arc brightens.
* The timer pill lifts slightly.
* A soft glass highlight fades in behind the icon for 150–200 ms.
* Then everything returns to normal.

This gives the feeling that the reward “comes alive” only when touched, instead of constantly sitting inside a box.

⸻

Remove the Card Border Entirely

Delete:

* Outer border
* Outer shadow
* Card outline

Keep only:

* Floating icon + progress arc
* Label
* Timer pill

The negative space itself becomes the design.

⸻

Keep the Pill

I actually like the timer pill.

I’d even make it feel more detached.

Instead of looking attached to a card, let it float underneath the icon with a little more vertical spacing.

⸻

Add a Soft Spotlight

Instead of a card:

Add an extremely subtle radial light behind the icon.

Almost like a spotlight on a stage.

Very low opacity.

It gives the icon presence without introducing another shape.

⸻

The Result

The hierarchy becomes:

      🎡
(progress arc)
DAILY SPIN
[18h 05m]

Nothing else.

Very elegant.

Very premium.

Very “Apple.”
