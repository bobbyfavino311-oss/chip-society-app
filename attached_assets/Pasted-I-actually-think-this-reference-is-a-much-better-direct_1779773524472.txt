I actually think this reference is a much better direction than where Chip Society currently is.

The biggest problem with the current Chip Society table is that it still feels like a *UI screen pretending to be a poker table*. The reference feels like an actual poker game because of the amount of empty space.

### What's better about this reference

**1. Massive negative space**

* 60-70% of the screen is empty
* Eyes immediately focus on the cards
* No visual clutter
* Feels premium

**Current Chip Society issue:**

* Too many neon elements
* Too many borders
* Too many labels
* Too many glowing containers

---

**2. Cards are the hero**
In the reference:

* Community cards dominate the center
* Cards are larger
* Nothing competes with them

In Chip Society:

* Pot
* Tournament overlays
* Blind labels
* Neon borders
* Status indicators

All fight for attention.

---

**3. Players feel secondary**
Reference:

Players are small and tucked away.

You only care about:

* Cards
* Pot
* Action

Chip Society currently gives equal visual weight to:

* Players
* Controls
* Cards
* Pot
* Background effects

Which creates noise.

---

**4. Cleaner action area**
Reference:

One simple action area.

Chip Society:

* Raise buttons
* Quick bet buttons
* Slider
* Pot shortcuts
* Multiple labels

Too much happening.

---
