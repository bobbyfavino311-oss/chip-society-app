# CRITICAL GAMEPLAY BUG — TRADITIONAL HOLD'EM HAND RANKING IS INCORRECT

There is still a serious hand-evaluation bug in Traditional Texas Hold'em.

In the reported hand:

• Player had a King-high Straight.
• Opponent had Two Pair: Jacks and Nines.
• The game incorrectly awarded the pot to the Two Pair hand.

This is wrong.

A Straight always beats Two Pair in standard Texas Hold'em.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

# CORRECT STANDARD HAND RANKING

Traditional Hold'em must use this exact order from highest to lowest:

1. Royal Flush
2. Straight Flush
3. Four of a Kind
4. Full House
5. Flush
6. Straight
7. Three of a Kind
8. Two Pair
9. One Pair
10. High Card

Therefore:

King-high Straight

beats

Jacks and Nines, Two Pair

every time.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

# REQUIRED FIX

Audit and repair the Traditional Hold'em hand evaluator.

The evaluator must:

• Generate the best possible five-card hand from the player's two hole cards and five community cards.
• Compare hand category first.
• Only compare kickers or card values when both players have the same hand category.
• Never compare a lower-ranked category as superior to a higher-ranked category.
• Never allow Two Pair to beat a Straight.
• Never reuse Short Deck ranking rules in Traditional Hold'em.
• Never reuse Joker Hold'em wildcard logic in Traditional Hold'em.
• Never apply Omaha's exact-two-hole-card rule in Traditional Hold'em.

Traditional Hold'em must use its own standard 52-card evaluator.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

# HAND-CATEGORY COMPARISON

The comparison engine must first assign each hand a category score.

Example:

Straight = category 5
Two Pair = category 3

The higher category must win before any rank detail is compared.

Do not compare:

• Pair values
• Kickers
• Hole-card strength
• Board contribution
• Preflop hand strength

until both hands are confirmed to be in the same category.

# STRAIGHT DETECTION

Verify straight detection across all valid combinations.

Examples:

K-Q-J-10-9 = King-high Straight

A King-high Straight must be stored as:

category: Straight
highCard: King

High Card
One Pair
Two Pair

# BEST FIVE-CARD HAND

Traditional Hold'em must evaluate all 21 possible five-card combinations from the seven available cards.

The winning hand must be the strongest valid five-card combination.

Do not stop evaluating after detecting a pair or two pair.

Example:

If the seven cards contain:

• Two Pair
and
• A Straight

The final hand is the Straight.

The evaluator must continue searching for the strongest category.

# SHARED BOARD HANDS

If the Straight exists entirely on the board:

• Both players may use it.
• Compare whether either player can improve beyond it.
• If neither improves, the hand is a split pot.

Do not award the hand based only on hole cards when the board already makes a stronger five-card hand.
